'''Finger Eleven''' is a Canadian [[Rock music|rock]] band from [[Burlington, Ontario]], formed in 1990. They have released seven studio albums (six as Finger Eleven and one as Rainbow Butt Monkeys), with their album ''[[The Greyest of Blue Skies]]'' bringing them into the mainstream. Their 2003 [[Finger Eleven (album)|self-titled album]] achieved Gold status in the United States and Platinum in Canada, largely from the success of the single "[[One Thing (Finger Eleven song)|One Thing]]", which marked the band's first placing on the US Hot 100 Chart at number 16. Their 2007 album, ''[[Them vs. You vs. Me]]'' launched the single "[[Paralyzer]]", which went on to top the Canadian Hot 100 and both US rock charts, as well as reaching No.&nbsp;6 on the US Hot 100 and No.&nbsp;12 on the Australian Singles Chart. They won the [[Juno Award]] for Rock Album of the Year in 2008.<ref>[http://junoawards.ca/08_nominees_cat.php?id=2327]{{dead link|date=November 2012}}</ref> The same album was later certified gold in the US and multi-platinum in Canada. They released their sixth studio album, ''[[Life Turns Electric]]'', on October 5, 2010; it was nominated as Best Rock Album of the Year at the [[Juno Awards of 2011]]. ''[[Five Crooked Lines]]'', their 7th studio album, was released in 2015. Between 1995 and 2016, Finger Eleven was among the top 75 best-selling Canadian artists in Canada and among the top 25 best-selling Canadian bands in Canada.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://bdsradio.com/pd/docs/Nielsen_Canadian150_2017_vs2.pdf|title=NIELSEN MUSIC & BILLBOARD PRESENT CANADA 150 CHARTS|website=bdsradio.com|page=28|access-date=January 2, 2020|archive-date=January 2, 2020|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20200102202137/https://bdsradio.com/pd/docs/Nielsen_Canadian150_2017_vs2.pdf|url-status=dead}}</ref> In 2023, the band released a compilation album titled ''Greatest Hits''.

==History==
===''Letters from Chutney'' (1990–1996)===
Finger Eleven first formed at [[Lester B. Pearson High School (Burlington)|Lester B. Pearson High School]]<ref>{{cite web |url=http://tourismburlington.com/about-burlington/famous-burlingtonians |title=Famous Burlingtonians |publisher=Tourismburlington.com |access-date=November 17, 2012 |archive-date=April 14, 2021 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20210414193336/https://tourismburlington.com/about-burlington/famous-burlingtonians/ |url-status=dead }}</ref> in [[Burlington, Ontario]] as Stone Soul Picnic, then changed their name to Rainbow Butt Monkeys. One of their first shows was at a school Christmas concert in 1990, and they won the [[CHTZ-FM|97.7 CHTZ (Hits) HTZ FM]]'s Rock Search competition. At the time, the band's lineup consisted of lead vocalist Scott Anderson, guitarists [[James Black (guitarist)|James Black]] and Rick Jackett, bassist Sean Anderson, and drummer Rob Gommerman.

They were signed by [[Mercury Records]] and, after releasing four EPs, they issued the album ''[[Letters from Chutney]]'' (1995), which received generally favourable reviews.<ref>[https://www.billboard.com/articles/news/67073/be-finger-eleven-funeral-for-a-friend "B&E: Finger Eleven, Funeral For A Friend."]. ''Billboard'', April 8, 2004</ref><ref>{{cite web |title=Rainbow Butt Monkeys |url=https://www.discogs.com/artist/1419895-Rainbow-Butt-Monkeys |website=discogs.com |publisher=Discogs |access-date=October 11, 2021}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |title=User Reviews for Letters from Chutney |url=https://www.allmusic.com/album/letters-from-chutney-mw0000525078/user-reviews |website=allmusic.com |publisher=AllMusic |access-date=October 11, 2021}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |title=Letters from Chutney Customer Reviews |url=https://www.amazon.com/Letters-Chutney-Rainbow-Butt-Monkeys/product-reviews/B00000736Y?reviewerType=all_reviews |website=amazon.com |publisher=Amazon |access-date=October 11, 2021}}</ref> In 1996, they were taken on by the management company Coalition Entertainment.<ref>{{cite web |title=Coalition Entertainment Founders Rob Lanni and Eric Lawrence, June 2009 |url=https://deanemediasolutions.com/coalition-entertainment-founders-rob-lanni-and-eric-lawrence/ |website=deanemediasolutions.com |date=July 10, 2009 |publisher=Deane Media Solutions |access-date=October 11, 2021}}</ref>

===''Tip'' (1997–1999)===
In 1997, the band changed its name to Finger Eleven, which came from a demo of their song "Thin Spirits".<ref>{{cite web |last1=Posthuma |first1=Brent |title=From Butt Monkeys to Finger Eleven, March 2007 |url=https://www.theinterrobang.ca/article?aID=2057 |website=theinterrobang.ca |publisher=Interrobang |access-date=October 11, 2021}}</ref> Their first album under the new name was ''[[Tip (album)|Tip]]'', produced by [[Arnold Lanni]] ([[Our Lady Peace]]).<ref>{{cite web |last1=Steininger |first1=Alex |title=Finger Eleven, Tip Review |url=https://www.inmusicwetrust.com/articles/12r09.html |website=inmusicwetrust.com |publisher=In Music We Trust |access-date=October 11, 2021}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |title=Finger Eleven, Tip, Review, Jan 2008 |url=https://www.sputnikmusic.com/review/14983/Finger-Eleven-Tip/ |website=sputnikmusic.com |publisher=Sputnik Music |access-date=October 11, 2021}}</ref> Mercury dropped the band after the album's release; they were then signed to [[Wind-up Records]], which re-released the record in 1998.<ref>{{usurped|1=[https://web.archive.org/web/20000413222441/http://www.chartattack.com/features/99/f11/index.html "FINGER ELEVEN: TAKING THEIR OWN TIP TO THE CROWDS"]}}. ''Chart Attack'', May 18, 1999, by James Hayashi-Tennant</ref> During this period, the band toured heavily, as part of [[Edgefest]], the Vans [[Warped Tour]] and, several times, opening for [[Creed (band)|Creed]]<ref>{{cite web |title=Creed heads south with Sevendust and others |url=https://www.antimusic.com/news/2000/oct/item22.shtml |website=antimusic.com |publisher=antiMUSIC |access-date=August 30, 2021}}</ref> and [[Fuel (band)|Fuel]].<ref>{{cite web |title=Finger Eleven Concert History |url=https://www.concertarchives.org/bands/finger-eleven?page=6#concert-table |website=concertarchives.org |publisher=Concert Archives |access-date=October 11, 2021}}</ref> In 1999, the track "Quicksand" was used in the TV series ''[[Roswell (TV series)|Roswell]]'' (uncredited).<ref>{{cite web |title=Finger Eleven Credits |url=https://www.imdb.com/name/nm1499070/#soundtrack |website=imdb.com |publisher=IMDB |access-date=October 11, 2021}}</ref>

Following the release of ''Tip'', drummer Rob Gommerman left the band. He was replaced by Rich Beddoe,<ref>{{cite web |last1=Budofsky |first1=Adam |title=Rich Beddoe of Finger Eleven |url=https://www.moderndrummer.com/2007/03/rich-beddoe/ |website=moderndrummer.com |date=March 2007 |publisher=Modern Drummer |access-date=October 11, 2021}}</ref> whom James Black had met at an [[Alice in Chains]] concert in Toronto several years earlier.

===''The Greyest of Blue Skies'' (2000–2002)===
In 2000, Finger Eleven released ''[[The Greyest of Blue Skies (album)|The Greyest of Blue Skies]]'',<ref>{{cite web |title=Finger Eleven – The Greyest Of Blue Skies |url=https://www.discogs.com/master/276551-Finger-Eleven-The-Greyest-Of-Blue-Skies |website=discogs.com |publisher=Discogs |access-date=October 11, 2021}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |last1=Tone |first1=Big |title=Finger Eleven The Greyest of Blue Skies |url=https://www.sputnikmusic.com/review/8219/Finger-Eleven-The-Greyest-of-Blue-Skies/ |website=sputnikmusic.com |publisher=Sputnik Music |access-date=August 30, 2021}}</ref> again produced by Arnold Lanni.<ref>[https://www.allmusic.com/album/the-greyest-of-blue-skies-mw0000070221 "Finger Eleven The Greyest of Blue Skies"]. ''AllMusic'', Review by David Reamer</ref> The album was commercially successful in Canada and was certified Gold.<ref>{{cite web |title=Gold Platinum Database - The Greyest of Blue Skies |url=http://www.musiccanada.com/GPSearchResult.aspx?st=the%20greyest%20of%20blue%20skies&ica=False&sa=finger%20eleven&sl=&smt=0&sat=-1&ssb=Cert.%20Date |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20120601104418/http://www.musiccanada.com/GPSearchResult.aspx?st=the%20greyest%20of%20blue%20skies&ica=False&sa=finger%20eleven&sl=&smt=0&sat=-1&ssb=Cert.%20Date |url-status=dead |archive-date=June 1, 2012 |publisher=Music Canada |access-date=October 11, 2021}}</ref> This was another period of heavy touring, with the band playing [[X-Fest]], [[Edgefest]], [[K-Rockathon]], and the four-city Canadian festival Summersault 2000. Finger Eleven also began appearing on its own, and headlining concerts with other bands.<ref>{{cite web |title=Finger Eleven Concert & Tour History |url=https://www.concertarchives.org/bands/finger-eleven?page=6#concert-table |website=concertarchives.org |publisher=Concert Archives |access-date=October 11, 2021}}</ref>

A song from this album, "Suffocate", was used in the 2000 film ''[[Scream 3]]''. In 2001, the song "Drag You Down" was used in the animated sitcom ''[[Daria]]''.<ref>{{cite web |title=Daria Fizz Ed Soundtracks |url=https://www.imdb.com/title/tt0554523/soundtrack |website=imdb.com |publisher=IMDB |access-date=October 11, 2021}}</ref> In 2002, Finger Eleven covered and remixed the entrance theme for wrestler [[Kane (wrestler)|Kane]], a song they called "Slow Chemical", which was recorded and mixed at [[Metalworks Studios]] in [[Mississauga, Ontario]]. Kane used the song from 2002 to 2008.<ref>{{cite web |title=WWE: Kane 3rd Theme "Slow Chemical" |url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=VNz-z7tgziI  |archive-url=https://ghostarchive.org/varchive/youtube/20211221/VNz-z7tgziI |archive-date=December 21, 2021 |url-status=live|website=youtube.com |publisher=YouTube |access-date=August 30, 2021}}{{cbignore}}</ref> It was also used in [[WrestleMania XX|WrestleMania 20]] (2004), [[WrestleMania 22]] (2006), [[WWE Backlash]] (2006), [[WrestleMania 23]] (2007), and in the video game ''[[WWE SmackDown vs. Raw 2008]]'',<ref>{{cite web |title=Finger Eleven Credits |url=https://www.imdb.com/name/nm1499070/ |website=imdb.com |publisher=IMDB |access-date=October 12, 2021}}</ref> as well as on the [[The Punisher: The Album|soundtrack]] for 2004's film ''[[The Punisher (2004 film)|The Punisher]]''.

===''Them vs. You vs. Me'' (2007–2009)===
<!-- Deleted image removed: [[File:FingerElevenliveinTempe.jpg|thumb|right|Scott Anderson performing with Finger Eleven in 2007]] -->

Finger Eleven's fifth studio album, ''[[Them vs. You vs. Me]]'', was released on March 6, 2007, with Johnny K returning as producer. The style on this album was more mellow than previous work.<ref>[https://www.straight.com/article-90226/screaming-gives-way-to-ranting-for-finger-eleven "Screaming gives way to ranting for Finger Eleven"]. ''Georgia Straight'', by Gregory Adams on May 9, 2007</ref> The lead single, "[[Paralyzer]]", became the band's biggest hit; it spent fifty weeks on the charts and reached No. 6 on the U.S. Hot 100 in November 2007,<ref>[https://www.thespec.com/news-story/2128356-finger-eleven-not-paralyzed-by-success/ "Finger Eleven not Paralyzed by success"]. ''Hamilton Spectator'', December 18, 2007</ref> as well as attaining the No.&nbsp;1 spot in Canada and on U.S. rock charts.<ref>{{cite web |title=Paralyzer Chart History |url=https://www.billboard.com/artist/finger-eleven/chart-history/hsi/ |website=billboard.com |publisher=Billboard |access-date=October 12, 2021}}</ref> The album also featured the singles "[[Falling On]]", "[[I'll Keep Your Memory Vague]]", and "[[Talking to the Walls]]".

"Paralyzer" was included in the soundtrack of the 2007 [[Victoria's Secret Fashion Show]],<ref>{{cite web |title=The Victoria's Secret Fashion Show |url=https://m.imdb.com/title/tt1179303/soundtrack/?ref_=tt_trv_snd |website=imdb.com |publisher=IMDB |access-date=October 12, 2021}}</ref> in a 2008 episode of the TV series ''[[Gossip Girl]]''<ref>{{cite web |title=Gossip Girl (2007) Sountrack |url=https://www.tunefind.com/show/gossip-girl/season-1/3161 |website=tunefind.com |publisher=Tunefind |access-date=October 12, 2021}}</ref> and, in 2009, in the soundtrack of the video game ''[[Band Hero]]''.

In December 2007, the DVD ''[[Us-vs-Then-vs-Now]]'' was released, with footage spanning the band's career up to that point.<ref>{{cite web |title=Finger Eleven – Us Vs. Then Vs. Now |url=https://www.discogs.com/master/1135655-Finger-Eleven-Us-Vs-Then-Vs-Now |website=discogs.com |publisher=Discogs |access-date=October 12, 2021}}</ref>

''Them vs. You vs. Me'' was certified Gold in the U.S. in March 2008, peaking at No. 14 on the Hot 100.<ref>{{cite web |title=Them Vs. You Vs. Me |url=https://www.billboard.com/music/finger-eleven/chart-history/RAL/song/523704 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20211030030057/https://www.billboard.com/music/finger-eleven/chart-history/RAL/song/523704 |url-status=dead |archive-date=October 30, 2021 |website=billboard.com |publisher=Billboard |access-date=October 12, 2021}}</ref> The album also won Rock Album of the Year at the [[Juno Awards of 2008]]. At the ceremony, the band performed live on national television with the Calgary Youth Orchestra.<ref>{{cite web |title=JUNO AWARDS 2008 - FINGER ELEVEN |url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=_xi-M_1IMvg  |archive-url=https://ghostarchive.org/varchive/youtube/20211221/_xi-M_1IMvg |archive-date=December 21, 2021 |url-status=live|website=youtube.com |publisher=YouTube |access-date=October 12, 2021}}{{cbignore}}</ref>

During this period, Finger Eleven continued to tour heavily, playing various festivals and numerous concerts with [[Seether]], [[3 Doors Down]], and [[Kid Rock]].<ref>{{cite web |title=Finger Eleven's Concert & Tour History |url=https://www.concertarchives.org/bands/finger-eleven?page=3#concert-table |website=concertarchives.com |publisher=Concert Archives |access-date=October 12, 2021}}</ref> They also performed at the 2007 NHL Awards Show in Toronto<ref>{{cite web |last1=Sandford |first1=Dave |title=2007 NHL Awards Show |url=https://www.gettyimages.ca/detail/news-photo/scott-anderson-of-finger-eleven-performs-onstage-during-the-news-photo/74630812 |website=gettyimages.ca |date=June 15, 2007 |publisher=Getty Images |access-date=October 12, 2021}}</ref> and, in 2009, played a show for Canadian and US troops in [[Kandahar]], Afghanistan<ref>{{cite web |title=Finger Eleven - One Thing live in Afghanistan |url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=3H63cT6wKEI  |archive-url=https://ghostarchive.org/varchive/youtube/20211221/3H63cT6wKEI |archive-date=December 21, 2021 |url-status=live|website=youtube.com |publisher=YouTube |access-date=October 12, 2021}}{{cbignore}}</ref>

===''Life Turns Electric'' (2010–2012)===
Finger Eleven's sixth album, ''[[Life Turns Electric]]'',<ref>{{cite web |title=Finger Eleven – Life Turns Electric |url=https://www.discogs.com/master/730490-Finger-Eleven-Life-Turns-Electric |website=discogs.com |publisher=Discogs |access-date=October 12, 2021}}</ref> was released in October 2010. The first single from the record, "[[Living in a Dream (Finger Eleven song)|Living in a Dream]]", was released on iTunes in July of that year; it added elements of funk rock and dance rock. A music video for the second single, "[[Whatever Doesn't Kill Me]]", was released in January 2011.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.reactivpictures.com/?alon/f11/|title=Reactive Pictures &#124; |publisher=Reactivpictures.com |date=April 22, 2011 |access-date=April 22, 2011}}</ref> The video was directed by [[Alon Isocianu]]. The remaining singles, "Stone Soul" and "Pieces Fit", were released without music videos. The album received mixed reviews.<ref>{{cite web |title=Finger Eleven electrifies with new album, Oct 2010 |url=https://www.oswegonian.com/2010/10/14/finger-eleven-electrifies-with-new-album/ |website=oswegonian.com |date=October 14, 2010 |publisher=The Oswegonian |access-date=October 12, 2021}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |title=Review: Finger Eleven Life Turns Electric |url=https://www.sputnikmusic.com/review/39481/Finger-Eleven-Life-Turns-Electric/ |website=sputnikmusic.com |publisher=Sputnik Music |access-date=October 12, 2021}}</ref> It peaked at No 88 on the Billboard Charts.<ref>{{cite web |title=Life Turns Electric, Finger Eleven |url=https://www.billboard.com/artist/finger-eleven/chart-history/tcl/ |website=billboard.com |publisher=Billboard |access-date=October 12, 2021}}</ref>

Finger Eleven toured in support of the record throughout 2011 and 2012, again playing numerous festivals and concerts with [[Seether]], [[Papa Roach]], and [[Three Days Grace]].<ref>{{cite web |title=Finger Eleven's Concert & Tour History |url=https://www.concertarchives.org/bands/finger-eleven?page=2#concert-table |website=concertarchives.org |publisher=Concert Archives |access-date=October 12, 2021}}</ref><ref>[https://leaderpost.com/entertainment/concert-review-finger-eleven "CONCERT REVIEW: Finger Eleven"]. ''Regina Leader-Post'', Jeff DeDekker, August 2, 2012</ref>

===''Five Crooked Lines'' (2013–2016)===
At the end of 2013, drummer Rich Beddoe left the band; he was replaced by Steve Molella<ref>{{cite web |last1=King |first1=Andrew |title=CM Catches Up With... Finger Eleven's Steve Molella @ CMW 2016 |url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=2Ebav4AqzzA  |archive-url=https://ghostarchive.org/varchive/youtube/20211221/2Ebav4AqzzA |archive-date=December 21, 2021 |url-status=live|website=youtube.com |publisher=Canadian Musician, YouTube |access-date=October 12, 2021}}{{cbignore}}</ref> the following year. In the interim, Finger Eleven recorded their next album with session drummer Chris Powell.<ref>{{cite web |title=Chris Powell Credits |url=https://www.allmusic.com/artist/chris-powell-mn0001448799/credits |website=allmusic.com |publisher=AllMusic |access-date=October 12, 2021}}</ref> Also in 2013, Wind-Up Records' catalogue was sold to the [[Bicycle Music Company]]<ref>{{cite web |last1=Christman |first1=Ed |title=Bicycle Music Acquires Wind-Up...Oct 2013 |url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/business/5777964/bicycle-music-acquires-wind-up-records-back-catalog-cuts |website=billboard.com |date=October 31, 2013 |publisher=Billboard |access-date=October 12, 2021}}</ref> which, in 2015, merged with [[Concord Music Group]], so that Finger Eleven's new label became Concord Bicycle Music.

The band toured during the summer of 2015 with [[Three Days Grace]].<ref>{{cite web |title=On the Road… with Three Days Grace and Finger Eleven |url=https://www.ghostcultmag.com/live-review-three-days-grace-finger-eleven-worcester-palladium/ |website=ghostcultmag.com |date=October 7, 2015 |publisher=Ghost Cult Magazine |access-date=October 12, 2021}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.pollstar.com/news_article.aspx?ID=819595|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20150804010253/http://www.pollstar.com/news_article.aspx?ID=819595|url-status=live|archive-date=August 4, 2015|title=We Apologize – Pollstar|work=pollstar.com}}</ref>
(In 2015, former Three Days Grace frontman [[Adam Gontier]] would team up with Beddoe, [[Staind]] guitarist [[Mike Mushok]], and bassist [[Corey Lowery]] to form the supergroup [[Saint Asonia]]).<ref>{{cite web |title=Former Three Days Grace frontman...May 2015 |url=https://www.altpress.com/news/former_three_days_grace_frontman_teams_with_staind_finger_eleven_members_fo/ |website=altpress.com |date=May 16, 2015 |publisher=AltPress |access-date=October 12, 2021}}</ref>

Finger Eleven issued their seventh album, ''[[Five Crooked Lines]]'', on July 31, 2015, and released the lead single "Wolves and Doors"<ref>{{cite web |title=Finger Eleven - Wolves and Doors (Music Video) |url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=UjNR_JMaVNc  |archive-url=https://ghostarchive.org/varchive/youtube/20211221/UjNR_JMaVNc |archive-date=December 21, 2021 |url-status=live|website=youtube.com |publisher=YouTube |access-date=October 12, 2021}}{{cbignore}}</ref> to Canadian radio. The album received favourable reviews, charted for one week, and reached No 44 on the charts.<ref>{{cite web |title=Five Crooked Lines, Finger Eleven |url=https://www.billboard.com/artist/finger-eleven/chart-history/rck/ |website=billboard.com |publisher=Billboard |access-date=October 12, 2021}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |last1=Jolicoeur |first1=Todd |title=CD REVIEW: FINGER ELEVEN – Five Crooked Lines, July 2015 |url=https://100percentrock.com/2015/07/cd-review-finger-eleven-five-crooked-lines/ |website=100percentrock.com |date=July 20, 2015 |access-date=October 12, 2021}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |last1=Tucker |first1=Brandon |title=Review: Finger Eleven "Five Crooked Lines", Aug 2015 |url=https://talknerdywithus.com/2015/08/review-finger-eleven-five-crooked-lines-2/ |website=talknerdywithus.com |date=August 3, 2015 |publisher=Talk Nerdy With Us |access-date=October 12, 2021}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |last1=Jones |first1=Patricia |title=Finger Eleven – Five Crooked Lines (Album Review), Aug 2015 |url=https://crypticrock.com/finger-eleven-five-crooked-lines-album-review/ |website=crypticrock.com |date=August 5, 2015 |publisher=Cryptic Rock |access-date=October 12, 2021}}</ref> They set out on their cross-Canada Fall of the Hammer Tour<ref>{{cite web |title=Finger Eleven Concerts |url=https://www.setlist.fm/stats/concert-map/finger-eleven-bd6b902.html?tour=bd6917e |website=setlist.fm |access-date=October 12, 2021}}</ref> in support of the release. In some markets, the band participated with media partners in an opening-act contest.<ref>{{cite web |title=FINGER ELEVEN ANNOUNCE ONTARIO DATES FOR "FALL OF THE HAMMER" TOUR |url=https://themusicexpress.ca/finger-eleven-announce-ontario-dates-for-fall-of-the-hammer-tour/ |website=themusicexpress.ca |date=October 7, 2015 |publisher=The Music Express |access-date=October 12, 2021}}</ref>

===Twentieth anniversary of ''Tip'' and Rainbow Butt Monkeys reunion (2017–2018)===
Finger Eleven scheduled three shows in southern Ontario to celebrate the twentieth anniversary of the Canadian release of ''Tip'', on March 23 in Burlington, March 24 in St. Catharines, and March 31 in Toronto. All three of these shows had a similar format: there was an exclusively acoustic set, with Q&A before the show. For the main event, the band played ''Tip'' in its entirety; this was followed by an encore of ''Best of the Rest'', a selection of band favourites from their other albums.{{Citation needed|date=October 2021}}

On June 9, 2018, Finger Eleven performed as Rainbow Butt Monkeys for the first time since 1997, playing at [[Burlington's Sound of Music Festival]] and showcasing songs from their debut album, ''Letters from Chutney''.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.insidehalton.com/whatson-story/8664703-rainbow-butt-monkeys-delivers-a-dose-of-nostalgia-at-burlington-sound-of-music-festival/|title=Rainbow Butt Monkeys delivers a dose of nostalgia at Burlington Sound of Music Festival|work=InsideHalton.com|date=June 11, 2018|access-date=August 29, 2018}}</ref> They have since played festivals and concerts throughout North America.<ref>{{cite web |title=Finger Eleven's Concert & Tour History |url=https://www.concertarchives.org/bands/finger-eleven |website=concertarchives.org |publisher=Concert Archives |access-date=October 12, 2021}}</ref>

===''Greatest Hits'', ''Last Night on Earth'' (2023–present)===
On May 10, 2023, the band released a music video for the song "Together Right", their first new material in over seven years, from their upcoming compilation album.<ref>{{cite news|title=Finger Eleven Releases Music Video for First New Single in More Than Seven Years, 'Together Right' |url=https://blabbermouth.net/news/finger-eleven-releases-music-video-for-first-new-single-in-more-than-seven-years-together-right |website=blabbermouth.net |date=May 10, 2023 |access-date=July 3, 2023}}</ref> ''Greatest Hits'' was issued on June 16 of the same year.<ref>{{cite news|title=Finger Eleven Release First-Ever Greatest Hits Collection This Summer |url=https://concord.com/news/finger-eleven-release-first-ever-greatest-hits-collection-this-summer/ |website=concord.com |access-date=September 6, 2023}}</ref>

In July 2024, Finger Eleven announced that their eighth studio album would be out in 2025, and in August, they released the first single, titled "Adrenaline".<ref>{{cite web|title=Finger Eleven Teases 'Adrenaline' Single; New Album Due in 2025 |url=https://blabbermouth.net/news/finger-eleven-teases-adrenaline-single-new-album-due-in-2025 |website=blabbermouth.net |date=July 17, 2024 |access-date=July 17, 2024}}</ref> In 2025, the band revealed that the new album would be called ''Last Night on Earth''.<ref>{{cite web|title=Finger Eleven Completes Work on First New Album in 10 Years, 'Last Night on Earth' |url=https://blabbermouth.net/news/finger-eleven-completes-work-on-new-album-last-night-on-earth |website=blabbermouth.net |date=May 31, 2025 |access-date=June 13, 2025}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|title=2000s rock band to release first new album in 10 years |url=https://www.syracuse.com/entertainment/2025/06/2000s-rock-band-to-release-first-new-album-in-10-years.html |website=syracuse.com |date=June 5, 2025 |access-date=June 13, 2025}}</ref>
