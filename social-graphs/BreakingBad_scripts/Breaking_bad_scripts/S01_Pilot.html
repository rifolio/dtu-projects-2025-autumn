About

Song Bio

In the premiere episode of what would later be regarded as one of the finest television shows of the 21st century, we see Bryan Cranston take his first steps towards breaking away from his comedic role of Hal in Malcolm In The Middle. For this reason alone the show had an impact off the bat, immediately saving Cranston from being pidgeonholed into the “funny dad” role and elevated him to one of the leading actors of our time.

The premise of a show about a good man who “breaks bad” was conceived by Vince Gilligan, after his time working on The X-Files. Gilligan said that he wanted to create a show in which the protagonist became the antagonist, or an antihero.

Television is historically good at keeping it’s characters in a self imposed stasis so that the show can go on for years, or even decades. When I realized this, the next logical step was to think, how can I do a show in which the fundamental drive is toward change?

Furthermore, Gilligan apparently joked, during his time in near unemployment, about driving around in an RV cooking meth with his fellow writer Thomas Schnauz. This of course fully conceptualized into what we know as Breaking Bad. And we can’t say that we’re mad about it, either.
