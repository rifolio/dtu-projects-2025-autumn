'''X Ambassadors''' (also stylized '''XA''') is an American [[pop rock]] band from [[Ithaca, New York]], that consists of lead vocalist Sam Harris, keyboardist Casey Harris, and drummer Adam Levin. Russ Flynn is a touring member who plays guitar and bass. Their most notable songs include "[[Jungle (X Ambassadors and Jamie N Commons song)|Jungle]]", "[[Renegades (X Ambassadors song)|Renegades]]", and "[[Unsteady (song)|Unsteady]]". The band's debut full-length album, ''[[VHS (album)|VHS]]'', was released on June 30, 2015. Their second album, ''[[Orion (X Ambassadors album)|Orion]]'', came out on June 14, 2019. Their third album, ''[[The Beautiful Liar (album)|The Beautiful Liar]]'', was issued on September 24, 2021. Their fourth album, ''[[Townie (album)|Townie]]'', came out on April 5, 2024.

==History==
===2009–12: ''Ambassadors'' and ''Litost''===
X Ambassadors began playing under the name Ambassadors, touring with artists such as [[Lights (musician)|LIGHTS]].<ref>{{Cite web |title=X Ambassadors Tours & Concerts |url=https://www.concertarchives.org/bands/x-ambassadors?page=44#concert-table |access-date=April 6, 2024 |website=Concert Archives}}</ref> During this time, they self-released their debut EP, ''Ambassadors'', creating a music video for its lead single, "Tropisms."<ref>{{Cite AV media |url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=As4UtXM_USs |title=Ambassadors "Tropisms" |date=November 17, 2009 |last=RethinkPopMusic |access-date=February 26, 2025 |via=YouTube}}</ref> Shortly after, they self-released their second EP, ''Litost'', which included the eponymous song, later to be used in the soundtrack for ''[[The Host (2013 film)|The Host]]''. The band signed a record deal in 2012 with SONGS Music Publishing.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.songspub.com/OneSheet/120/X%20Ambassadors |title=SONGS Music Publishing |website=Songspub.com |access-date=April 29, 2016 |archive-date=April 25, 2016 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160425210242/https://www.songspub.com/OneSheet/120/X%20Ambassadors |url-status=dead }}</ref> They launched a [[Kickstarter]] campaign to fund and shoot two music videos for the EP's singles, "Weight/Lightness" and "Unconsolable", with the latter being re-recorded later.<ref>{{cite web|url = https://www.kickstarter.com/projects/1611117510/help-ambassadors-make-two-music-videos|title = Help Ambassadors Make Two Music Videos! by Jacob Albert — Kickstarter|publisher = Kickstarter.com|access-date = July 23, 2014}}</ref> The group was noticed by [[Imagine Dragons]] while frontman [[Dan Reynolds (musician)|Dan Reynolds]] was sick in the hospital in Norfolk, Virginia. Reynolds heard an acoustic version of "Unconsolable" on [[WROX-FM|96X WROX-FM]] and asked [[Interscope Records|Interscope]] to sign the band as soon as possible.<ref name="X Ambassadors discuss signing with major label, new EP, and influences">{{cite web|url=http://pigeonsandplanes.com/2013/05/x-ambassadors-unconsolable/ |title=Introducing: X Ambassadors, Brooklyn's Least "Indie" Rock Band {{pipe}} Pigeons & Planes |publisher=Pigeonsandplanes.com |access-date=July 23, 2014}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=yy_lMxe7B5E&t=3m16s |title=X Ambassadors Interview – 92 WICB |date=November 10, 2015 |publisher=YouTube |access-date=April 29, 2016}}</ref>

===2013–14: ''Love Songs Drug Songs'' and ''The Reason''===
X Ambassadors released their debut major-label EP, ''[[Love Songs Drug Songs]]'', in 2013. It included the track "Stranger", co-written by Dan Reynolds.<ref>{{cite web|author = Matt Collar|url = http://www.allmusic.com/album/love-songs-drug-songs-mw0002529177|title = Love Songs Drug Songs – X Ambassadors {{pipe}} Songs, Reviews, Credits, Awards|publisher = AllMusic|date = May 7, 2013|access-date = July 23, 2014}}</ref> In promotion of the album, they toured in support of Imagine Dragons, [[Jimmy Eat World]], and [[the Mowgli's]].<ref>{{Cite web |author=A. O. L. Staff |title=X Ambassadors gets candid about their relationship with Imagine Dragons |url=https://www.yahoo.com/2016-03-08-x-ambassadors-gets-candid-about-their-relationship-with-imagine-21324127.html |access-date=April 6, 2024 |website=yahoo.com}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web |title=X Ambassadors |url=https://bunburyfestival.com/lineup-2014/bands/ambassadors |access-date=April 6, 2024 |website=Bunbury Music Festival |archive-date=April 6, 2024 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20240406235821/https://bunburyfestival.com/lineup-2014/bands/ambassadors |url-status=dead }}</ref><ref>{{Cite web |title=Jimmy Eat World ★ Mainroom |url=https://first-avenue.com/event/jimmy-eat-world/ |access-date=April 6, 2024 |website=First Avenue}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web |last=Law |first=Tarynn |date=September 30, 2013 |title=The Mowgli's announce November tour with X Ambassadors |url=https://idobi.com/news/the-mowglis-announce-november-tour-with-x-ambassadors/ |access-date=April 6, 2024 |website=idobi}}</ref> In 2014, the band released their next EP, ''[[The Reason (EP)|The Reason]]''. Two of its tracks, "[[Unsteady (song)|Unsteady]]" and "[[Jungle (X Ambassadors and Jamie N Commons song)|Jungle]]", appeared on their full-length album ''VHS'', released after a year. In promotion of the new EP, X Ambassadors supported [[Panic! at the Disco]] and Imagine Dragons on their respective tours.<ref>{{Cite web |title=X Ambassadors tour w/ Panic! at the Disco & Imagine Dragons {{!}} Big Smile Magazine |url=https://www.readbsm.com/article/x-ambassadors-tour-w-panic-at-the-disco-imagine-dragons/c-5GEGZEKPmMOAAaCUWA0eCk |access-date=April 6, 2024 |website=readbsm.com |archive-date=April 6, 2024 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20240406235822/https://www.readbsm.com/article/x-ambassadors-tour-w-panic-at-the-disco-imagine-dragons/c-5GEGZEKPmMOAAaCUWA0eCk |url-status=dead }}</ref>

===2015–16: ''VHS''===
In March 2015, X Ambassadors released the single "[[Renegades (X Ambassadors song)|Renegades]]", which appeared on their debut album, "[[VHS (album)|VHS]]", published on June 30, 2015, and featuring collaborations with [[Jamie N Commons]] and Imagine Dragons. A special edition, titled ''VHS 2.0'' was released on June 10, 2016, with five additional tracks and without the interludes that were included on the original version.

The band collaborated with [[the Knocks]] on the song "Comfortable", from the duo's debut album, ''[[55 (album)|55]]'', released in March 2016.

On June 20, 2016, X Ambassadors announced that Feldshuh would be taking an indefinite hiatus from the band to deal with personal issues.<ref>{{cite web|title=X Ambassadors' Noah Feldshuh on Indefinite Hiatus from Band|url=http://kroq.radio.com/2016/06/17/x-ambassadors-noah-feldshuh-takes-indefinite-hiatus/|website=KROQ|access-date=January 18, 2018|date=June 17, 2016}}</ref>

===2017: Single releases===
X Ambassadors released four standalone singles in 2017: "Hoping" in March, "Torches" in April, "The Devil You Know" in June, and "[[Ahead of Myself]]" in July.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=l-1H1iJp5uw|title=X Ambassadors Nervous About Second Album! – Exclusive Interview|last=iHeartRadio|date=July 26, 2017|access-date=December 25, 2017|publisher=[[YouTube]]}}</ref> They also performed at the [[2017 National Scout Jamboree]]. The band was featured in a song by [[Eminem]] titled "Bad Husband", from his album ''[[Revival (Eminem album)|Revival]]''. They also collaborated on "[[Home (Machine Gun Kelly, X Ambassadors and Bebe Rexha song)|Home]]", a song from the soundtrack for the Netflix film ''[[Bright (film)|Bright]]''. The track includes pop singer [[Bebe Rexha]] and rapper [[Machine Gun Kelly (musician)|Machine Gun Kelly]].

===2018–2020: ''Joyful'' (cancelled album), ''Orion'', and ''Belong''===
X Ambassadors released their next single, "Joyful", on January 26, 2018, originally intended to be the title track of their second record.<ref>{{Cite magazine|url=https://www.billboard.com/music/rock/x-ambassadors-ahead-of-myself-video-interview-7965564/|title=X Ambassadors Explain Inspiration for 'Ahead of Myself' Video, Label Trump 'An Idiot' Over DACA|magazine=Billboard|access-date=January 22, 2018}}</ref> Pre-orders of the album became available through their website later that day,<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.broadwayworld.com/bwwmusic/article/X-Ambassadors-Release-Powerful-New-Single-Video-Joyful-20180126|title=X Ambassadors Release Powerful New Single + Video 'Joyful'|author=BWW News Desk|access-date=January 27, 2018}}</ref> and it was slated for an April 2018 release. On February 2, 2018, the band issued another single, titled "Don't Stay".<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://music.apple.com/us/album/dont-stay-single/1340648148|title=Don't Stay – Single by X Ambassadors on Apple Music|work=Apple Music|access-date=February 2, 2018}}</ref>

On January 24, 2019, the group put out their next single, "[[Boom (X Ambassadors song)|Boom]]". Three months later, on April 19, 2019, over a year after the announcement of ''Joyful'', the band stated they had cancelled it in favor of new material for a different record, to be titled ''[[Orion (X Ambassadors album)|Orion]]'', which would include the previously released track "Boom". Vocalist Sam Harris stated that ''Joyful'' was the second iteration of their second album and that they felt like the songs on it did not represent where they were at.<ref name="orion">{{Cite magazine|url=https://www.billboard.com/music/rock/x-ambassadors-sam-harris-interview-hey-child-single-orion-album-8507835/|title=X Ambassadors' Sam Harris Talks 'Hey Child' Single, 'Game of Thrones' Music & Rock's Urgent Need for Diversity|magazine=Billboard|access-date=April 19, 2019}}</ref> That same day, the band issued the song "[[Hey Child]]" as the second single off ''Orion''. A third single, titled "[[Hold You Down (X Ambassadors song)|Hold You Down]]", was published on May 31, 2019.<ref>{{cite web|author=X AMBASSADORS (@XAmbassadors)|date=May 30, 2019|url=https://twitter.com/XAmbassadors/status/1134157939399307265|title=Tomorrow #HoldYouDown|publisher=[[Twitter]]}}</ref> The album came out on June 14, 2019.<ref name="orion" />

On April 19, 2019, American rapper and singer [[Lizzo]] released her album "[[Cuz I Love You]]", which featured the tracks "Cuz I Love You", "Jerome", and "Heaven Help Me", all produced by X Ambassadors.<ref>{{cite web |last1=Hampp |first1=Andrew |title=Songs for Screens: X Ambassadors' Sam Nelson Harris Talks 'Game of Thrones,' Playing Paul Stanley of Kiss |url=https://variety.com/2019/music/news/x-ambassadors-sam-nelson-harris-synch-game-of-thrones-kiss-1203301410/ |website=Variety |date=August 13, 2019 |access-date=December 6, 2019}}</ref> The band also wrote the song "Baptize Me" with singer Jacob Banks for the ''[[Game of Thrones]]'' compilation ''For the Throne''.

On August 16, 2019, X Ambassadors were featured on a song titled "[[In Your Arms (Illenium and X Ambassadors song)|In Your Arms]]", from [[Illenium]]'s third studio album, ''[[Ascend (Illenium album)|Ascend]]''.<ref>{{cite web|author=X AMBASSADORS (@XAmbassadors)|date=August 16, 2019|url=https://twitter.com/XAmbassadors/status/1162408208578727936|title=.@ILLENIUMMUSIC "ASCEND" out NOW go listen, thank you for having us on "In Your Arms"|publisher=Twitter}}</ref>

On August 23, 2019, the band released a politically charged standalone single called "Optimistic", which centers around gun violence in the United States.<ref>{{cite web|date=August 23, 2019|url=https://twitter.com/XAmbassadors/status/1164948555452182529|title=We need to keep these conversations going. About gun violence prevention. The 24-hour news cycle is unrelenting, but it's important we stay vigilant and don't forget these injustices.|publisher=Twitter}}</ref>

On February 7, 2020, they issued the song "Everything Sounds Like a Love Song",<ref>{{cite web|author=X AMBASSADORS (@XAmbassadors)|date=February 7, 2020|url=https://twitter.com/XAmbassadors/status/1225645361332654080|title=Everything Sounds Like a Love Song out now|publisher=Twitter}}</ref> promoted as the lead single from their forthcoming EP, ''Belong''. On February 21, 2020, the band put out a single titled "Great Unknown" for the film ''[[The Call of the Wild (2020 film)|The Call of the Wild]]''.<ref>{{cite web|author=X AMBASSADORS (@XAmbassadors)|date=February 17, 2020|url=https://twitter.com/XAmbassadors/status/1229521723772428288|title=GREAT UNKNOWN. THIS FRIDAY. #CalloftheWild|publisher=Twitter}}</ref>

''Belong'' came out on March 6, 2020, and included the title track as well as "Everything Sounds Like a Love Song" and "Happy Home". Sam Harris stated that it was a collection of tracks the band wrote that didn't quite fit on their upcoming third studio album, but they loved the songs and wanted to put them out.<ref>{{cite web |last1=Bocci |first1=Maria |title=X Ambassadors on belonging, brotherhood and musicianship |url=https://www.thelineofbestfit.com/features/interviews/x-ambassadors-on-belonging-brotherhood-musicianship |website=The Line of Best Fit |access-date=March 5, 2020}}</ref> On May 15, 2020, X Ambassadors released another single, "[[Zen (X Ambassadors, K.Flay, and Grandson song)|Zen]]", with [[K.Flay]] and [[Grandson (musician)|grandson]], about the [[COVID-19 pandemic]].

===2021–2022: ''(Eg)'', ''The Beautiful Liar''===
On January 14, 2021, X Ambassadors released a single called "ultraviolet.tragedies", a heavily rap-focused song featuring [[Terrell Hines]]. It was the first single from the collaborations project ''(Eg)''. Sam Harris said that with this project, he wanted to "give a voice to (several) incredible artists that people may not have heard yet, and really let their imagination be at the forefront of everything".<ref>{{Cite web|title=X Ambassadors announce collaborative '(Eg)' project; listen to new single "ultraviolet.tragedies" now – Music News – ABC News Radio|url=http://abcnewsradioonline.com/music-news/2021/1/15/x-ambassadors-announce-collaborative-eg-project-listen-to-ne.html|access-date=February 14, 2021|website=abcnewsradioonline.com}}</ref> On February 7, 2021, the band released a cover of "[[Blinding Lights]]" by [[the Weeknd]].

On February 12, 2021, they released the next single from ''(Eg)'', "skip.that.party", featuring [[Jensen McRae]]. They issued one more single, "torture", on March 12, 2021, featuring [[Earl St. Clair]].

On June 11, 2021, the band put out a song titled "My Own Monster", which was also announced to be the lead single from their third studio album, ''[[The Beautiful Liar (album)|The Beautiful Liar]]''. "Okay" was released as the second single on July 30, 2021, while "Adrenaline" came out on August 13, 2021. The album was published on September 24, 2021, along with a music video for the song "Beautiful Liar". A tour for the record began in October.<ref>{{cite web|author=Vevo (@Vevo)|date=June 18, 2021|url=https://twitter.com/Vevo/status/1405918501680807938|title=.@XAmbassadors' fall tour is booked and their third album 'The Beautiful Liar' is on the way. Until then we got their eerie new video for "My Own Monster" on repeat. Watch here.|publisher=Twitter}}</ref> ''The Beautiful Liar'' is a [[concept album]] that plays out similarly to a radio drama, expressing themes of insanity, powerlessness, self-doubt, and anxiety.<ref>{{Cite web|title=X-Ambassadors lose their minds as they strive to reclaim success on 'The Beautiful Liar'|url=https://www.cavalierdaily.com/article/2021/10/x-ambassadors-lose-their-minds-as-they-strive-to-reclaim-success-on-the-beautiful-liar|access-date=October 2, 2021|website=The Cavalier Daily – University of Virginia's Student Newspaper}}</ref> It tells the story of a blind girl whose shadow comes to life. It has a [[Trap music|trap]]-influenced sound, a contrast from the band's previous releases, and tells its story over the course of 16 tracks. Sam and Casey said that the album was influenced by radio shows that they listened to as children. About the record, Sam said, "I wanted to make an album that felt as unhinged as I did, and as the whole world felt around me. So Adam, Casey and I carved ourselves a space where we could all be as ugly, weird, funny, and f'd up as we wanted to be."<ref>{{Cite web|last=Paul|first=Larisha|date=September 24, 2021|title=X Ambassadors Share New Album 'The Beautiful Liar' {{!}} uDiscover|url=https://www.udiscovermusic.com/news/x-ambassadors-the-beautiful-liar-album-release/|access-date=October 2, 2021|website=uDiscover Music}}</ref>

On October 22, 2021, the band released "Water", a song for the ''[[Blade Runner: Black Lotus]]'' soundtrack. The band was featured on "You'll Get Yours" from [[Tom Morello]]'s album ''[[The Atlas Underground Flood]]'', released December 3, 2021. On May 12, 2022, they issued a remix of their song "Palo Santo", featuring ROBI.

On December 9, 2022, the band collaborated with Belgian DJ [[Lost Frequencies]] and singer [[Elley Duhé]] on "[[Back to You (Lost Frequencies, Elley Duhé, and X Ambassadors song)|Back to You]]". A music video for the song was released on January 19, 2023.<ref>{{Citation |title=Lost Frequencies, Elley Duhé, X Ambassadors – Back to You (Official Video) | date=January 19, 2023 |url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=eXBNFf7CF9U |access-date=March 23, 2023}}</ref> A deluxe pack was released on March 10, 2023, containing remixes from various DJs.

===2023–present: ''(Eg)'' season two, ''Townie'', ''Townies'', ''VHS(X)''===
[[File:X_Ambassadors_in_Sydney.jpg|thumb|right|X Ambassadors performing in Sydney, Australia, in January 2025]]

On May 24, 2023, X Ambassadors released "Happy People", featuring [[Teddy Swims]] and Jac Ross, starting season two of ''(Eg)''. The next single, released on June 23, was "Friend for Life", featuring [[Medium Build]], followed by "Devastation", featuring PAMÉ, released on September 22, and "Alcohol", featuring [[Breland (musician)|Breland]], released on October 27.

On November 17, 2023, the band put out the song "Deep End", for the soundtrack to ''[[Aquaman and the Lost Kingdom]]''.

In late 2023, X Ambassadors began teasing snippets of songs from their upcoming fourth studio album, ''[[Townie (album)|Townie]]''. The record, published on April 5, 2024, was preceded by four singles: "No Strings", released on January 19, 2024; "Your Town", released on February 16, 2024; "Half-Life", released on March 15, 2024; and "Follow the Sound of My Voice", released on March 29, 2024.<ref>{{Cite web |author=Wonderland Staff |date=January 19, 2024 |title=X Ambassadors – "No Strings" |url=https://www.wonderlandmagazine.com/2024/01/19/x-ambassadors-no-string/ |access-date=January 19, 2024 |website=Wonderland}}</ref> The band released the EP ''[[Townies (EP)|Townies]]'' alongside the single "Fragile" on October 15.

In January 2025, X Ambassadors opened for [[the Script]] on the Australian leg of their Satellites World Tour.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.frontiertouring.com/news/x-ambassadors-usa-announce-exclusive-headline-melbourne-show-for-january-2025|title=X Ambassadors (USA) announce exclusive headline Melbourne show for January 2025|publisher=Frontier Touring|date=September 26, 2024|accessdate=February 2, 2025}}</ref>

In July, the band announced that to celebrate the tenth anniversary of its release, they would be issuing a reimagined edition of their debut album, ''VHS'', titled ''VHS(X)''. It is set to come out on August 29, and the trio will promote it with a tour.<ref>{{cite web|title=For the 10th Anniversary of Their Debut Album, X Ambassadors Deliver 'VHS(X)' with Reworked Tracks |url=https://wildfiremusic.net/2025/07/04/for-the-10th-anniversary-of-their-debut-album-x-ambassadors-deliver-vhsx-with-reworked-tracks/?utm_source=perplexity |website=wildfiremusic.net |date=July 4, 2025 |access-date=August 9, 2025}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|title=X Ambassadors Drop VHS(X) and Tour for Album's 10th Anniversary |url=https://nysmusic.com/2025/08/05/x-ambassadors-drop-vhsx-and-tour-for-albums-10th-anniversary/?utm_source=perplexity |website=nysmusic.com |date=August 5, 2025 |access-date=August 9, 2025}}</ref>

Sam Harris has stated that all of the members grew up listening to hip hop and R&B, but they also appreciated rock music, specifically alternative and indie groups.<ref>{{Cite web |date=January 29, 2018 |title=X Ambassadors: Straight Outta Ithaca |url=https://www.musicconnection.com/x-ambassadors/ |access-date=April 7, 2024 |website=Music Connection Magazine}}</ref> While writing their second album, he cited [[Soul music|soul]] influences, including [[Ray Charles]], [[Joe Cocker]], [[Otis Redding]], and [[the Band]].<ref>{{Cite web |title=X Ambassadors – Bio |url=https://traceypepper.com/past-writing/bios/x-ambassadors/ |access-date=April 7, 2024 |website=Tracey Pepper |archive-date=April 7, 2024 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20240407001326/https://traceypepper.com/past-writing/bios/x-ambassadors/ |url-status=dead }}</ref> It has often been noted that Casey Harris being [[Visual impairment|blind]] has been a major part of shaping the band's music.<ref>{{Cite news|last=Andy Thorley|date=September 28, 2021|title=Review: X-Ambassadors – The Beautiful Liar (2021)|url=https://maximumvolumemusic.com/review-x-ambassadors-the-beautiful-liar-2021/|access-date=October 2, 2021|website=Maximum Volume Music}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web |date=June 13, 2019 |title=X Ambassadors Frontman Sam Harris Unwraps the Rich History and Influences Behind 'Orion' |url=https://entertainmentvoice.com/2019/06/13/x-ambassadors-frontman-sam-harris-unwraps-the-rich-history-and-influences-behind-orion/ |access-date=April 7, 2024 |website=Entertainment Voice}}</ref>

==Solo projects==
On May 5, 2023, Sam Harris released his debut solo album, ''Why Does Everything Make Me Cry?'', under the name Sam Nelson.<ref>{{Cite web |title=Sam Nelson – YouTube |url=https://www.youtube.com/post/Ugkxi6aBufv4NrCD4DY2NtkHENbrYvRd0utW |access-date=May 5, 2023 |website=youtube.com}}</ref> It contains three singles: "Forever, Now", "Battery Acid", and "Give Me Hell", the latter featuring [[Madi Diaz]].

==Philanthropy==
In January 2017, Sam Harris participated in the official Women's March on Washington after-party.<ref>{{Cite magazine|url=https://www.rollingstone.com/culture/news/the-national-sleater-kinney-tig-notaro-join-planned-parenthood-benefit-w461543|title=The National, Sleater-Kinney, Tig Notaro Join Planned Parenthood Benefit|magazine=Rolling Stone|access-date=September 6, 2017}}</ref><ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.washingtonpost.com/news/reliable-source/wp/2017/01/22/an-abundance-of-performers-and-pussyhats-at-womens-march-after-party/|title=An abundance of performers and pussyhats at Women's March after-party|last1=Polus|first1=Sarah|date=January 22, 2017|newspaper=The Washington Post|access-date=September 6, 2017|last2=Polus|first2=Sarah|issn=0190-8286}}</ref> He was joined by [[The National (band)|the National]], [[Ani DiFranco]], [[Samantha Ronson]], [[Sleater-Kinney]], and many others.

In March 2017, X Ambassadors performed a special show to benefit [[Planned Parenthood]] on International Women's Day.<ref>{{Cite magazine|url=https://www.billboard.com/music/rock/x-ambassadors-planned-parenthood-benefit-concert-sam-harris-interview-7701865/|title=X Ambassadors' Sam Harris on Supporting Planned Parenthood with Benefit Show: 'It's Such a Vital Organization'|magazine=Billboard|access-date=September 6, 2017}}</ref>

Following the benefit show, the band released the song "Hoping" on March 10.<ref>{{Citation|title=Hoping – Single by X Ambassadors on Apple Music|date=March 10, 2017|url=https://music.apple.com/us/album/hoping-single/1444624906|access-date=September 6, 2017}}</ref><ref name="auto">{{Cite magazine|url=https://www.billboard.com/music/rock/x-ambassadors-new-song-hoping-aclu-listen-7718024/|title=X Ambassadors Debut New Song 'Hoping,' Benefitting the ACLU: Listen|magazine=Billboard|access-date=September 6, 2017}}</ref> All proceeds from the song for the six months following its release were donated to the ACLU.<ref name="auto"/>

In June 2017, X Ambassadors announced that they would donate all the proceeds from their Mississippi Coast Coliseum Show to Unity Mississippi—an LGBT charitable organization,{{citation needed|date=July 2023}} following the announcement that the state of Mississippi signed HB 1523 – the "Protecting Freedom of Conscience from Government Discrimination Act" into law.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.allaccess.com/net-news/archive/story/155178/x-ambassadors-donate-proceeds-from-local-gig-to-lg|title=X Ambassadors Donate Proceeds from Local Gig to LGBT Organization Unity Mississippi|work=All Access|access-date=September 6, 2017}}</ref>
