'''The Script''' are<!-- 
 "is". --> an Irish [[pop rock]] band formed in 2001 in [[Dublin]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.allmusic.com/artist/the-script-mn0001013375/biography|title=The Script Biography|publisher=[[Allmusic]]|access-date=1 March 2017}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.sputnikmusic.com/review/36503/The-Script-The-Script/ |title=The Script ‚Äì The Script (album review) |publisher=Sputnikmusic |date=28 April 2010 |access-date=7 November 2012}}</ref> The band currently consists of [[Danny O'Donoghue]] (lead vocals, guitar, piano, keyboards), Glen Power (drums, percussion, backing vocals), Benjamin Seargent (bass, backing vocals) and Ben Weaver (guitar). [[Mark Sheehan]] (guitar, vocals) was a member of the band up until his death in 2023. The band moved to [[London]] after signing to [[Sony Music Entertainment|Sony Label Group]] imprint [[Phonogenic Records|Phonogenic]] and released their first album [[The Script (album)|''The Script'']] in August 2008, preceded by the debut single "[[We Cry]]" as well as other singles such as "[[The Man Who Can't Be Moved]]", "[[Breakeven (song)|Breakeven]]" and "[[Before the Worst]]". The album peaked at number one in both Ireland and the UK.<ref name="BBC2010"/> Their next three albums, ''[[Science & Faith]]'' (2010), ''[[3 (The Script album)|#3]]'' (2012) and ''[[No Sound Without Silence]]'' (2014), all topped the album charts in Ireland and the UK, while ''Science & Faith'' reached number two in Australia<ref name="AusScriptAlbums">{{cite web|url=https://acharts.co/album/57298|title=SCIENCE & FAITH|publisher=ACharts|accessdate=12 February 2023}}</ref> and number three in the United States.<ref name="BBC2010">[https://www.bbc.co.uk/news/entertainment-arts-11364771 The Script score fresh chart success with new album]. BBC. Retrieved 27 April 2012</ref><ref name=":0">[https://www.billboard.com/articles/news/473403/decemberists-king-is-dead-is-no-1-on-billboard-200 Decemberists' 'King Is Dead' Is No.&nbsp;1 on Billboard 200]. ''Billboard''. Retrieved 27 April 2012</ref> Some of the hit singles from the albums include "[[For the First Time (The Script song)|For the First Time]]", "[[Nothing (The Script song)|Nothing]]", "[[Hall of Fame (song)|Hall of Fame]]" and "[[Superheroes (song)|Superheroes]]". The band's fifth studio album, ''[[Freedom Child]]'', was released on 1 September 2017, and features the UK Top 20 single "[[Rain (The Script song)|Rain]]". Their sixth studio album, ''[[Sunsets & Full Moons]]'', was released on 8 November 2019, and features the single "[[The Last Time (The Script song)|The Last Time]]". A [[Tales from the Script: Greatest Hits|Greatest Hits]] album was released on 1 October 2021.<ref name="greatesthits2021"/>
 
The Script's music has been featured in television programmes such as ''[[90210 (TV series)|90210]]'', ''[[Ghost Whisperer]]'', ''[[The Hills (TV series)|The Hills]]'', ''[[Waterloo Road (TV series)|Waterloo Road]]'', ''[[EastEnders]]'', ''[[Made in Chelsea]]'' and ''[[The Vampire Diaries (TV series)|The Vampire Diaries]]''. Frontman Danny O'Donoghue was also a coach on ''[[The Voice UK]] ''for seasons 1 and 2, before leaving the show in order to focus more on the band.<ref>{{cite web| title = Danny O'Donoghue and The Voice| publisher = BBC| date = 16 July 2013| url =https://www.bbc.co.uk/mediacentre/latestnews/2013/danny-the-voice.html| archive-url =https://web.archive.org/web/20130718224110/http://www.bbc.co.uk/mediacentre/latestnews/2013/danny-the-voice.html| url-status =dead| archive-date =18 July 2013|access-date=1 November 2014}}</ref> The band has won three [[Meteor Ireland Music Awards]] and two [[World Music Awards]] and have received two [[Brit Awards|Brit Award]] nominations. The Script have sold over 20 million albums worldwide.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://tap1.dk/the-script-koebenhavn/?lang=en|title=THE SCRIPT TIL K√òBENHAVN!|access-date=11 August 2019}}</ref>

On 14 April 2023, band co-founder [[Mark Sheehan]] died of a brief illness at the age of 46.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.independent.ie/entertainment/the-script-guitarist-mark-sheehan-has-died-in-hospital-42433320.html|title=The Script guitarist Mark Sheehan has died in hospital|publisher=[[Irish Independent]]|first1=Barry|last1=Egan|first2=Gareth|last2=Morgan|date=14 April 2023|accessdate=14 April 2023}}</ref>

==History==

===Early years===
Danny O'Donoghue and Mark Sheehan had been best friends since they were 12 growing up in [[Dublin]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.theguardian.com/tv-and-radio/2012/may/27/danny-odonoghue-script-voice-interview |title=Danny O'Donoghue: 'I idolised Dad. I'd bring home my songs to him like paychecks' |last=Day |first=Elizabeth |work=The Guardian |date=27 May 2012 |access-date=1 November 2014 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20120601213610/http://www.guardian.co.uk/tv-and-radio/2012/may/27/danny-odonoghue-script-voice-interview |archive-date=1 June 2012 }}</ref><ref name="The Script">{{cite web|url=http://www.theargus.co.uk/leisure/music/10293649.print/|title=The Script|date=20 March 2013 |publisher=The Argus|access-date=11 July 2013}}</ref> They were formerly part of a boy band called [[Mytown]], formed in 1996. Striking up a songwriting and production partnership, they were invited to Canada to collaborate with some of their heroes, including [[Dallas Austin]], [[Montell Jordan]] and [[Teddy Riley (producer)|Teddy Riley]].<ref name="AllMusic">{{cite web|url=https://www.allmusic.com/artist/the-script-mn0001013375|title=The Script ‚Äì Music Biography, Credits and Discography : AllMusic|work=[[AllMusic]]|access-date=15 July 2013}}</ref> They were in the US for a few years and got a record deal until the company split and dropped them.

They stayed in [[Los Angeles]] and worked there as producers, writing and producing songs for performers including [[Britney Spears]], [[Boyz II Men]] and [[TLC (group)|TLC]],<ref name="The Script"/> but later moved back to Dublin, where they recruited drummer Glen Power for their new band the Script. Power learned to play drums when he was just eight and he had been playing sessions from the age of 15, using the money to work on a solo project in his home, but that went on hold when his collaboration with Sheehan and O'Donoghue produced three songs in one week.{{original research inline|date=February 2013}} The band signed to Phonogenic in 2005, and released an EP on [[Last.FM]]. The band's influences include [[Snow Patrol]], [[U2]], [[Coldplay]], [[the Police]], [[the Neptunes]], [[Timbaland]], and [[Van Morrison]].<ref name="AllMusic"/>

===2008‚Äì2010: ''The Script''===
The Script's eponymous debut album was released on 8 August 2008.<ref name="Independent">{{cite news|url=http://www.independent.ie/entertainment/music/oh-danny-boy-script-revel-in-chart-success-26470692.html|title=Oh Danny boy! Script revel in chart success|work=The Independent|access-date=12 July 2013|date=26 November 2012}}</ref> The album debuted at the number one spot on the UK's album charts.<ref>[https://news.bbc.co.uk/2/hi/entertainment/7566743.stm Entertainment {{!}} The Script crown UK album chart]. BBC News (17 August 2008). Retrieved on 3 March 2014.</ref>

The Script performed "We Cry" for the first time on [[BalconyTV]] in Dublin on 13 September 2007.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.balconytv.com/Video-Archive/Video-Archive-page/07-09-12/THE_SCRIPT.aspx |title=Video Archive |publisher=Balcony TV |access-date=7 November 2012 |url-status=usurped |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20100407081004/http://www.balconytv.com/Video-Archive/Video-Archive-page/07-09-12/THE_SCRIPT.aspx |archive-date=7 April 2010 }}</ref> The band claimed that BalconyTV was the "first TV they ever did" and credited Michael Hunter as the  "push we needed to propel us" when they won Best Band at the DanishTV Music Video Awards 2009 in the Pepper Club Dublin on 20 June 2008. At the awards the band said the award was their "first ever".<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=6KI6yFUsj2I&feature=fvsr | archive-url=https://ghostarchive.org/varchive/youtube/20211107/6KI6yFUsj2I| archive-date=2021-11-07 | url-status=live|title=The Script Win Best Band | date=September 2008|publisher=YouTube |access-date=7 November 2012}}{{cbignore}}</ref> The Script released their debut single "[[We Cry]]" on 25 April 2008. "We Cry" received "Single of the Day" on [[RT√â 2fm|RT√â 2FM]], [[Today FM]] and by [[Jo Whiley]] on [[BBC Radio 1]], where many radio presenters heavily supported the band.<ref name="rca">{{cite news| title=RCA Label Group (UK) / News / The Script ‚Äì We Cry the Single of the Week| publisher=Rcalabelgroup.co.uk| url=http://www.rcalabelgroup.co.uk/news/8842/0/| access-date=26 March 2008| url-status=dead| archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20080327112533/http://www.rcalabelgroup.co.uk/news/8842/0/| archive-date=27 March 2008| df=dmy-all}}</ref> The single peaked at number 15 on the [[UK Singles Chart]],<ref name="The Script ‚Äì We Cry ‚Äì Music Charts">{{cite web|url=http://acharts.us/song/35195 |title=The Script ‚Äì We Cry ‚Äì Music Charts |publisher=Acharts.us |access-date=9 February 2009}}</ref> giving the band their first top 20 single. The track also performed well on the [[Irish Singles Chart]], peaking at number 9 and giving the band their first top ten single in their home country.<ref name="The Script ‚Äì We Cry ‚Äì Music Charts"/> The Script also performed "We Cry" on one of the CW's TV series, ''[[90210 (TV series)|90210]]''.

Their second single, "[[The Man Who Can't Be Moved]]", was released on 15 September 2008.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.thescriptmusic.com/gb/music/ |title=The Script |publisher=Thescriptmusic.com |access-date=9 February 2009 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20090216202346/http://www.thescriptmusic.com/gb/music/ |archive-date=16 February 2009 |df=dmy-all }}</ref> The single reached number 2 in Ireland, Denmark, and the UK.<ref>{{cite magazine|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/news/1044358/the-script-perry-in-charge-on-uk-charts|title=The Script, Perry In Charge On U.K. Charts|magazine=Billboard|publisher=Nielsen Business Media, Inc|access-date=1 December 2009}}</ref> The band released their debut album, ''[[The Script (album)|The Script]]'', on 11 August 2008. Following the success of "The Man Who Can't Be Moved", the album entered the UK Albums Chart at number one with sales of 20,240 copies where it stayed for 11 weeks. The album spent three weeks in the top ten and was the 18th best selling album in the UK of 2008. The album also entered the [[Irish Albums Chart]] at number one, holding the top spot for five weeks.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://acharts.us/album/37191 |title=The Script ‚Äì The Script ‚Äì Music Charts |publisher=Acharts.us |date=7 January 2008 |access-date=9 February 2009}}</ref> The Irish office of Sony BMG music presented the group with their first multi-platinum disc for over 600,000 sales of their debut album.<ref name="Sony">{{cite web|url=http://www.sony.com/SCA/company-news/press-releases/epic-records/2012/multi-platinum-dublin-rockers-the-script-release-t.shtml |title=Multi-Platinum Dublin Rockers The Script Release Third Album Today; First Single "Hall of Fame" (featuring will.i.am) Inspires Global Audience |publisher=Sony |access-date=12 July 2013 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20130702190816/http://www.sony.com/SCA/company-news/press-releases/epic-records/2012/multi-platinum-dublin-rockers-the-script-release-t.shtml |archive-date=2 July 2013 }}</ref>

The band's third single, "[[Breakeven (song)|Breakeven]]", was released in Ireland and UK on 21 November 2008 and 29 December 2008 respectively. The single was an instant success on the [[Irish Singles Chart]]. After entering the chart at number 40, it spent one week before entering the top ten at number 10, giving the Script their third top ten single in Ireland. The single spent four weeks on the UK Singles Chart, peaking at number 21. The band's next two singles, "Talk You Down"<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.amazon.co.uk/Talk-You-Down-Script/dp/B002YP4F3W/ref=sr_1_2?s=music&ie=UTF8&qid=1333953847&sr=1-2|title=Talk You Down ‚Äì Amazon|publisher=Amazon|access-date=12 July 2013}}</ref> and "[[Before the Worst]]",<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.amazon.com/Before-The-Worst/dp/B001J9GHTE|title=Before the Worst ‚Äì Amazon|publisher=Amazon|access-date=12 July 2013}}</ref> were released in March and July 2009, respectively.

On 4 October, the Script performed "Before the Worst" at half-time of the [[2009 NRL Grand Final]] in Sydney, Australia, in their first visit to the country.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.nrl.com/news/2009/09/27/grand-final-entertainment-program/|title=Grand Final entertainment program|publisher=[[National Rugby League]]|date=27 September 2009|accessdate=20 November 2022}}</ref>

On 9 November the Script received an award at the [[World Music Awards]] for Best-Selling Irish Act of 2008 and on 19 February 2010, they won Best Live Performance at the [[Meteor Ireland Music Awards]].

The band played at the Cheerios Childline concert, headlined by [[Enrique Iglesias]] and [[Anastacia]], on the opening night of Dublin's [[3Arena (Dublin)|The O2]] in 2008.<ref>{{cite news |last1=Cunningham |first1=Grainne |last2=Cody |first2=Caitrina |title=O2 Rocks to Sound of Music |url=https://www.independent.ie/entertainment/music/o2-rocks-to-sound-of-music-26500135.html |access-date=4 September 2021 |work=Independent.ie |date=17 December 2008}}</ref> The Script supported [[Take That]] at their concert at [[Croke Park]] on 13 June 2009. On 27 July, the band supported [[U2]] on their [[U2 360¬∞ Tour|third concert]] at Croke Park.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.setlist.fm/setlist/the-script/2009/croke-park-dublin-ireland-63d726b3.html|publisher=Setlist.fm|title=The Script Setlist|access-date=11 July 2013}}</ref> On 17, 18 and 21 July 2009, the Script opened for [[Paul McCartney]] at the first concerts at New York's [[Citi Field]].<ref name="ReferenceB">{{cite web|url=http://www.thescriptmusic.com/ie/live/archive/us/ |title=Gig Archive‚ÄîThe Script |publisher=Thescriptmusic.com |access-date=7 November 2012}}</ref>

The band's popularity reached the attention of other artists. In March 2008, the band announced they planned to write a track for [[Leona Lewis]]'s second album, [[Echo (Leona Lewis album)|''Echo'']],<ref>{{cite web |title=The Script to write track for Leona Lewis |publisher=Digital Spy| url=http://www.digitalspy.co.uk/music/a123451/the-script-to-write-track-for-leona-lewis.html |date=16 March 2008 |access-date=23 November 2008}}</ref> but in 2010, Sheehan revealed that they had "missed the mark" on the album's release date and that they submitted their song too late. While Sheehan believed Lewis's record company loved their song, he said, he felt "they thought it was a little dark for her".<ref>{{cite web |last1=Love |first1=Ryan |title=The Script: Leona Track Was Too Dark |url=https://www.digitalspy.com/music/a281005/the-script-leona-track-was-too-dark/ |website=Digital Spy |access-date=4 September 2021 |date=10 August 2010}}</ref>

The band's bonus track and B-side "[[Live Like We're Dying]]" was re-recorded by [[American Idol]] 2009 winner [[Kris Allen]] as the first single from his self-titled album.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.musicloversgroup.com/kris-allen-live-like-were-dying-lyrics-and-video/ |title=Kris Allen ‚Äì Live Like We're Dying Lyrics and Video |publisher=Musicloversgroup.com |date=17 September 2009 |access-date=7 November 2012}}</ref>

A remixed version of the band's single "[[The Man Who Can't Be Moved]]" was featured during the [[Victoria's Secret Fashion Show 2009]].<ref>{{cite web |url=http://vsholic.com/music/victorias-secret-fashion-show-music-2009/ |publisher=Vsholic.com |title=Victoria's Secret 2009 |access-date=11 July 2013 |url-status=usurped |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20110530023813/http://vsholic.com/music/victorias-secret-fashion-show-music-2009 |archive-date=30 May 2011}}</ref>

On 16 April 2010, their first US single "Breakeven" was certified Platinum by the [[Recording Industry Association of America]], selling over one million copies in the US; the song peaked at No.&nbsp;12 on the ''Billboard'' Hot 100 in the spring of 2010. The band performed a headline tour in the United States that kicked off in San Diego on 11 October 2010 at the [[House of Blues]] and ended on 7 November 2010 at the House of Blues in Boston, Massachusetts.<ref name="ReferenceB"/>

===2012‚Äì2014: ''#3''===
The band's third album was titled ''[[3 (The Script album)|#3]]''. It was recorded in Los Angeles, Studio 3, and released on [[iTunes]] on 21 August 2012. The single features [[will.i.am]], whom Danny works alongside on ''[[The Voice UK]]''. The song became their most successful to date, being certified 5√ó Platinum in Australia, Platinum in Italy, 2√ó Platinum in New Zealand, 6√ó Platinum in Norway, 2√ó Platinum in Sweden and 2√ó Platinum in the US. ===2014‚Äì2016: ''No Sound Without Silence''===
[[File:The Script NSWS Tour.JPG|thumb|right|The band performing on their ''No Sound Without Silence'' tour in Barcelona, Spain in 2015]]
The band's fourth studio album was titled ''[[No Sound Without Silence]]'' and the lead single from the album, "[[Superheroes (The Script song)|Superheroes]]", was released on 22 July 2014.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.lyrics.com/lyric/31555189/The+Script/Superheroes|title=The Script - Superheroes |website=www.lyrics.com}}</ref> The album has been described by O'Donoghue as a prequel to their debut album, with many of the songs being recorded on the road.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.rte.ie/ten/news/2014/0718/631719-the-script-announce-new-album-details/|title=The Script announce new album details|publisher=rte.ie|access-date=1 November 2014}}</ref> The album was released on 12 September 2014.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.independent.ie/style/celebrity/celebrity-news/the-script-reveal-they-miss-superquinn-sausages-and-chipper-chips-the-most-while-on-international-tours/30583501.html|title=The Script reveal they miss Superquinn sausages and 'chipper chips' the most while on international tours|date=12 September 2014|website=Independent.ie}}</ref>

Other singles from the album include "[[No Good in Goodbye]]", which was released on 15 October 2014 and "[[Man on a Wire (The Script song)|Man on a Wire]]" which was released on 15 March 2015.

The band's tour for this album. titled the "[[No Sound Without Silence Tour]]" occurred from 16 January 2015 through 18 September 2015. Throughout the tour, the band played a total of 56 total shows, and performed within Africa, Asia, Europe, Oceania, and North America.

Immediately after the tour, the band went on a nearly year-long unannounced hiatus. O'Donoghue didn't reveal this information until July 2017 and claimed that the hiatus occurred due to the band wanting some time off to "chill" after making four albums and going on five world tours.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.officialcharts.com/chart-news/the-script-have-released-a-new-single-called-rain-and-we-had-a-chat-with-them-about-it-its-a-case-of-adapt-and-change-or-die__19655/|title=The Script have released a new single called Rain and we had a chat with them about it: "It's a case of adapt and change or die"|publisher=Official Charts|access-date=14 July 2017}}</ref> Shortly after this announcement, it was revealed that another reason the band went on hiatus was due to O'Donoghue needing throat surgery.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.standard.co.uk/showbiz/celebrity-news/the-scripts-danny-o-donoghue-on-speaking-for-the-first-time-after-vocal-surgery-it-was-an-emotional-a3599076.html|title=The Script's Danny O'Donoghue on speaking for the first time after vocal surgery: 'It was an emotional experience'|date=28 July 2017 |publisher=EveningStandard|access-date=28 July 2017}}</ref>

===2017‚Äì2019: ''Freedom Child''===
Beginning in early 2017, the band began teasing their new fifth album. They did so by releasing a couple of sneak peeks of songs from the album throughout the year. A sneak peek of a song titled "Divided States of America" was released on 5 January 2017.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=NvvjomS-g-A| archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20171120122902/https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=NvvjomS-g-A| archive-date=2017-11-20 | url-status=dead|title=THE SCRIPT - UNITE #thescriptfamily|publisher=s7 vlog|access-date=5 January 2017}}</ref> A few months later, a sneak peek of a song titled "Written in the Scars" was released on 8 June 2017.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Cr__tsYLU3E| archive-url=https://ghostarchive.org/varchive/youtube/20211107/Cr__tsYLU3E| archive-date=2021-11-07 | url-status=live|title=Written in the Scars by The Script (New Teaser)| date=8 June 2017|publisher=Raden Ayu Andini|access-date=8 June 2017}}{{cbignore}}</ref> On 13 July 2017, the band officially announced the lead single from the album, "[[Rain (The Script song)|Rain]]". The song was released on 14 July 2017.<ref>{{Cite web|author=The Script (@thescript)|date=13 July 2017|url=https://twitter.com/thescript/status/885408483847294977|title=Rain}}</ref> On 21 July 2017, it was announced that the new album would be titled ''[[Freedom Child]]''. The album was put up for pre-order that same day. It contains 14 tracks and was released on 1 September 2017.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.thescriptmusic.com/|title=The Script - Freedom Child|publisher=The Script|access-date=21 July 2017}}</ref>

Prior to the album's release, two promotional singles were released to further promote the album. On 25 August 2017, "Written in the Scars" was released as the first promotional single from the album. A few days later, on 30 August 2017, "Love Not Lovers" was released as the second promotional single from the album.

Upon release, the album received mixed to negative reviews from critics upon release, but still managed top the charts in some countries. The album hit number one in Ireland, Scotland, and the United Kingdom, their third album to do so.

The song "Arms Open" was released as the second single from the album on 13 November 2017. A music video for the song was released on 1 December 2017.

The tour for the album, simply titled "Freedom Child Tour", took place between September 2017 and June 2018.

On 16 March 2018, the band released a live cover of the [[Aslan (band)|Aslan]] song "[[Crazy World (Aslan song)|Crazy World]]" featuring [[Christy Dignam]], the lead singer of Aslan. This single was featured on an acoustic EP titled ''Acoustic Sessions'', which also feature the songs "No Man is an Island", "Never Seen Anything 'Quite Like You'", and "The Man Who Can't Be Moved". The full EP was released on 18 April 2018.

===2019‚Äì2021: ''Sunsets & Full Moons''===
Beginning on 13 September 2019, the band began teasing a new album on Twitter. A few days later, on 19 September 2019, the band announced the lead single from the album, "[[The Last Time (The Script song)|The Last Time]]". The single and an accompanying music video were released on 20 September 2019.<ref>{{Cite web|author=The Script (@thescript)|date=19 September 2019|url=https://twitter.com/thescript/status/1174579091430957058|title=As if we'd never even been away ü§òüèª #TheLastTime. Out tomorrow. Danny, Mark & Glen}}</ref><ref>{{Citation|title=The Last Time|url=https://open.spotify.com/album/7hKlP3f3yyQjizcUIKWvZk|language=en|access-date=2019-09-19}}</ref>

On 11 October 2019, the band announced that the album would be titled ''[[Sunsets & Full Moons]]'' and consist of nine songs. The album was made available for pre-order the same day. It was released on 8 November 2019.<ref>{{Cite web|author=The Script (@thescript)|date=11 October 2019|url=https://twitter.com/thescript/status/1182551406525370369|title=Excited to announce our new album 'Sunsets & Full Moons' is out November 8th If you live in the UK and Ireland, get exclusive access to our 2020 tour ticket pre-sale on Weds October 16th, by pre-ordering the album before 5pm on Tuesday!}}</ref>

On 1 November 2019, "Something Unreal" was released as the first promotional single from the album.

In December 2019, The Script performed at the [[Jingle Bell Ball]], alongside other artists such as [[Ava Max]], [[Rita Ora]], [[Regard (DJ)|Regard]] and [[Mabel (singer)|Mabel]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.capitalfm.com/events/jingle-bell-ball/2019-full-line-up-artists-stars-performing/|title=Capital's Jingle Bell Ball With SEAT Line-Up: Taylor Swift & Stormzy Are Playing The UK's Biggest Christmas Party|publisher=Capital FM|date=4 November 2019|accessdate=12 November 2022}}</ref>

On 10 January 2020, "Run Through Walls" was released as the second single from the album. A music video for the song was released the same day.

On 11 March 2021, the band released an acoustic version of "For the First Time", which would serve as the lead single off of their second acoustic EP titled ''Acoustic Sessions 2''. The remainder of the EP was released on 17 March 2021.

===2021‚Äì2023: ''Tales from the Script: Greatest Hits'' & Mark Sheehan's death===
[[File:The_Script_performing_in_Sydney.jpg|thumb|right|The Script performing in Sydney, Australia in September 2022.]]
Beginning in late August 2021, the band began teasing a new song on their website and Twitter page. The song, "I Want It All", was released on 27 August along with an announcement for a greatest hits album, ''[[Tales from the Script: Greatest Hits]]''. The announcement set the album's release date for 1 October 2021;<ref name="greatesthits2021">{{cite web |last1=Miller |first1=Mark |title=The Script announce new career-spanning greatest hits album ''Tales From The Script'' ‚Äì Out October 1st |url=https://www.xsnoize.com/the-script-announce-new-career-spanning-greatest-hits-album-tales-from-the-script-out-october-1st/ |website=XS Noize |access-date=5 September 2021 |date=27 August 2021}}</ref> a worldwide tour was also announced in support of the album, beginning in [[Los Angeles]] on 30 March 2022 and concluding in Lisbon, Portugal on 18 November 2022.<ref name="2022tour1">{{cite web|url=https://www.nme.com/news/music/the-script-announce-2022-tour-for-uk-and-europe-detail-tales-from-the-script-compilation-3031821|title=The Script announce 2022 tour for UK and Europe, detail ''Tales from the Script'' compilation|work=[[NME]]|last=Doria|first=Matt|date=29 August 2021|access-date=5 October 2021}}</ref><ref name="2022tour2">{{cite web|url=https://musicfeeds.com.au/news/the-script-announce-australian-tour-for-2022/|title=The Script Announce Headline Australian Tour for 2022|publisher=Music Feeds|first=Alex|last=Gallagher|date=30 September 2021|accessdate=19 November 2022}}</ref><ref name="2022tour3">{{cite web|url=https://vocalbop.com/the-script-greatest-hits-tour-2022-dates-in-usa-uk-and-australia-tickets-guidelines/|title=The Script 'Greatest Hits Tour 2022' Dates In USA, UK And Australia : Tickets Guidelines|publisher=Vocal Bop|first=Foysal|last=Patwary|date=5 October 2021 |accessdate=19 November 2022}}</ref>

In November 2021, the Script performed at Hits Live in Liverpool, alongside other artists such as [[Mimi Webb]], Mabel, [[Becky Hill]], [[Joel Corry]], [[Tom Grennan]], [[Ella Henderson]] and [[Ed Sheeran]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://planetradio.co.uk/hits-radio/entertainment/music/hits-live-line-up-2021/|title=Hits Live 2021: Who performed at the shows?|publisher=Planet Radio|first=Natalie|last=Rees|date=31 January 2022|accessdate=18 November 2022}}</ref>

On 10 February 2022, the band released an acoustic version of "I Want It All", which would serve as the lead single off of their third acoustic EP titled ''Acoustic Sessions 3''. "Superheroes" was released as the second single from the EP on 11 March 2022. The remainder of the EP was released on 17 March 2022. The remaining songs that are included are "Paint the Town Green" and "Good Ol' Days".

The band toured in 2022. Mark Sheehan missed the U.S. leg of the tour. Danny O'Donoghue later attributed Sheehan's absence to family commitments.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.independent.co.uk/arts-entertainment/music/news/the-script-danny-odonoghue-mark-sheehan-b2350702.html|title=The Script frontman Danny O'Donoghue breaks silence on death of Mark Sheehan|date=2 June 2023|website=The Independent}}</ref>

On 14 April 2023, lead guitarist and backing vocalist [[Mark Sheehan]] died at aged 46 in hospital after a brief illness.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.irishtimes.com/culture/music/2023/04/14/mark-sheehan-guitarist-with-the-script-dies-aged-46/|title=Mark Sheehan, guitarist with The Script, dies aged 46|publisher=[[The Irish Times]]|first=Glen|last=Murphy|date=14 April 2023|accessdate=14 April 2023}}</ref>

===2024‚Äìpresent: ''Satellites''===

On 8 May 2024, the band announced a new single titled "Both Ways" on X, their first single since Sheehan's death. The following day, they announced that long-time supporting tour member Ben Sargeant and newcomer Ben Weaver would be joining the band as their bassist and lead guitarist, respectively, as they felt being a trio ‚Äúwould never be the same‚Äù. The single was released on 17 May 2024.

The second single off the album, titled "At Your Feet", was released on 19 July 2024.

Their seventh studio album, titled ''[[Satellites (The Script album)|Satellites]]'', was released on 16 August 2024. They are embarking on a world tour in support of the album, kicking off in the United States of America (where they are also opening for [[Pink (singer)|Pink]] on her [[Summer Carnival (tour)|Summer Carnival Tour]]) and also traversing through Europe, Australia and Asia''.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.thescriptmusic.com/|title=The Script|accessdate=17 August 2024}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.hotpress.com/music/the-script-announced-as-special-guests-for-pnks-north-american-tour-22999132|title=The Script announced as special guests for P!NK's North American tour|publisher=Hot Press|first=Ricciardo|last=Dwyer|date=5 December 2023|accessdate=17 August 2024}}</ref>''
